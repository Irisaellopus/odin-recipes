Adobo

Filipino-style chicken adobo

Adobo is derived from the spanish word adobar, meaning marinade. The practice of marinating meat in a flavorful misture made from vinegar, salt, garlic, paprika, and oregano was common to Spanish cooking.

Spanish colonists gave the name “adobo” to the cooking method indigenous to the Philippines, as their marinades were so similar. Today, Spanish adobo typically refers to the sauce, an intensely flavored and red, due to the paprika,containing chipotle chiles that have been stewed in the basic adobo marinade mixture.

Ingredients

  • 8 whole chicken legs (about 4 pounds), cut into drumstick and thigh sections
  • 1 1/2 cups distilled white vinegar
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 tablespoon whole black peppercorns, crushed lightly
  • 3/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • cooked rice as an accompaniment

Steps

  1. In a large kettle combine the chicken, the vinegar, the garlic, the bay leaves, the peppercorns, and 1 cup water, bring the mixture to a boil, and simmer it, covered, for 20 minutes.
  2. Add the soy sauce and simmer the mixture, covered, for 20 minutes. Transfer the chicken with tongs to a plate and boil the liquid for 10 minutes, or until it is reduced to about 1 cup.
  3. Let the sauce cool, remove the bay leaves, and skim the fat from the surface.
  4. In a large skillet heat the oil over high heat until it is hot but not smoking and in it sauté the chicken, patted dry, in batches, turning it, for 5 minutes, or until it is browned well.
  5. Transfer the chicken to a rimmed platter, pour the sauce, heated, over it, and serve the chicken with the rice.
